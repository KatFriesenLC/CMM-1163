People

Latin American and Mexico as we know it today has been indoctrinated with a very eurocentric way of life. The Spanish language itself was something brought over by Los Conquistadores, the Spanish colonial conquerors. Long ago now, the area was home to the indigenous people groups: Lenca, Maya Chorti, Maya Pocomam, Cacaopera, and Nahua Pipil. The Pipils are a migrant Nahua speaking group from central Mexico and they make up a majority of the indigenous population still living in El Salvador.

The history of El Salvador is one filled with triumph and tribulation. The people have survived the battles of colonization and the terrors of a civil war as recent as 1980-1992. These events have had lasting effects on the people and culture in the country and their resiliency is seen in the way they live. Passionate and loud in their love and generous in hardship.
